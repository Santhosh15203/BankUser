  • Banks accept deposits and give loans.
  • Types of Banks: Commercial, Cooperative, RRBs, Development, Central Bank (RBI).
  • Main Functions: Accepting deposits, giving loans, fund transfers, ATM, digital banking.
  • RBI (Reserve Bank of India) controls and regulates all banks in India.
  • Types of Accounts: Savings, Current, Fixed Deposit (FD), Recurring Deposit (RD).
  • Loans Provided: Home, personal, education, business, vehicle, gold loans, etc.
  • Digital Services: UPI, mobile banking, net banking, debit/credit cards.
  • PM Jan Dhan Yojana aims at financial inclusion for every household.
  • Deposits insured up to ₹5 lakh by DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation).
  • Key Banking Laws: Banking Regulation Act 1949, RBI Act 1934, Negotiable Instruments Act 1881.
  • KYC (Know Your Customer) is mandatory for opening a bank account.
  • IFSC Code is used for identifying branches in electronic money transfer systems.
  • NEFT/RTGS/IMPS are electronic payment modes used for fund transfer.
  • Banking Ombudsman helps customers resolve complaints against banks.
  • NPA (Non-Performing Assets) are loans where borrowers default on repayment.
  • Scheduled vs Non-Scheduled Banks – based on inclusion in the Second Schedule of the RBI Act.
  • Green Banking refers to banking practices that aim to protect the environment.
  • SWIFT Code is used for international banking transactions.
